JGS Foods founder and namesake, Jeri Guss Spitzer
Jeri’s Gourmet & Bar-B-Que Sauce is a proud product of Utah. Locally owned & operated, the Guss family has produced Jeri’s Sauce in Salt Lake City for more than 40 years. No other sauce can match our taste or quality. Jeri’s Sauce is a concentrate. It can be used straight or diluted with water, beer, wine, orange juice, cider, or soda. The ingredients are purely natural and are the highest quality available on the market today. There are no preservatives, artificial colorings or flavors, extenders, fillers, or dilutants. Jeri’s does not contain any fats or added salts. The flavor varies with the diluting agent and adapts to the meal, be it meat, fowl, fish, or game. Jeri’s may also be used as a base for cold dips, hot appetizers, salad dressings, marinades, glazes, as well as a table condiment, and of course, as a conventional bar-b-que sauce. Jeri’s is a very unique product, and because of our focus on quality, cannot be compared to the less expensive, diluted, watered-down commercially available sauces sold for less. Jeri’s Gourmet & Bar-B-Que Sauce is presently featured on the menus of many fine hotels, resorts, and elegant restaurants throughout Utah. Classic Jeri's Recipes
Jeri's Thousand Island Dressing:
  • 3 cups Salad Dressing or Mayonnaise
  • 1 cup Jeri's Sauce
  • 1/3 cup Pickle Relish (sweet or plain)
  • Mix ingredients. Store in refrigerator until needed.
Jeri's Cold Dip:
  • 1/3 cup Jeri's Sauce
  • Dairy Sour Cream or Plain Yogurt
  • Seasoning Salt of your choice (Italian seasoning, curry powder, season salt, etc. - optional)
  • Assorted raw vegetables, chips, crackers - even cheese cubes!
  • Combine equal parts of Jeri's Sauce and Sour Cream or Yogurt. Stir in Seasoning Salt to taste and serve in a bowl. Arrange vegetables, chips crackers or cheese cubes around bowl. Dip and enjoy!
Jeri's Baked Beans:
  • 2 small or 1 large can Pork and Beans
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1/3 cup Brown Sugar
  • 1/3 cup Molasses
  • 2/3 Jeri’s Sauce
  • 1/2 tsp. Salt
  • 1/2 Tbs. Pepper
  • Combine all ingredients in a 2 quart casserole. Bake at 350degrees until desired thickness.
Jeri’s Style Teenie Weiners:
  • Weiners or Cocktail Weiners
  • Beer or Water
  • Jeri's Sauce
  • Cut weiners into bite size pieces (if not already). Add equal parts Jeri's sauce and beer/water into a pot. Be sure the sauce covers the weiners! Cook on stove or in crock pot until done.
Nachos Jeri’s Style:
  • Round Nacho Chips
  • One 8oz can Refried Beans
  • 1/3 cup Jeri's Sauce
  • American or Mild Cheddar Cheese
  • Green Onions
  • Jalapeno Peppers
  • Mix Jeri's Sauce with beans - spread on chips. Chop green onions on top of beans, fold 1/2 slice American or Cheddar Cheese on top of beans. Slice peppers and place on top of cheese. Place on broiler or in Microwave until cheese melts.
Jeri’s Chicken:
  • Wine or Beer
  • Jeri's Sauce
  • Whole or Cut up Chicken
  • Flour
  • Cut up chicken (if not already), dip in flour and place in shallow pan. Bake in oven at 400°. When chicken is almost to your liking, add equal parts of wine or beer and Jeri's Sauce together and brush on chicken. Cook for 10 to 15 minutes, turn - cook and brush other side for another 10 to 15 minutes.
  • For a quick snack, simply broil chicken wings or pieces in the oven to desired doneness on one side, turn and coat with Jeri's Sauce concentrate and broil an additional 5-10 minutes.
Jeri's Sauce Shrimp and Chicken:
  • 1/4 cup Butter or Margarine
  • 1 cup chopped Onion
  • 2 tsp. Salt
  • 1/2 tsp. Pepper
  • 1/4 cup Port Wine
  • 1 tsp. Basil
  • 1/4 Parsley
  • 1/2 cup Jeri's Sauce
  • 3 medium Chicken Breasts, cut in half
  • 1 lb. deveined shelled Shrimp
  • In a large skillet melt butter and sauté onion. Set aside. Rub chicken with salt and pepper, then brown in butter remaining in skillet. Stir in Jeri's Sauce, basil, wine, 3 Tbsp. parsley and sautéed onion. Heat and boil, then reduce to simmer for 10 minutes or until chicken is tender. Add shrimp, bring to boil, then reduce to simmer for 2 to 3 minutes. Stir often. Serve in deep dish and top with remaining parsley.
Jeri’s Sauce Vegetable and Chip Dip:
  • 1/3 cup Jeri's Sauce
  • 1/3 to 1/2 cup Sour Cream, IMO or Plain Yogurt
  • Add spices to taste (season salt, curry powder, italian seasonings, etc.)
  • Combine all ingredients, mix well and refrigerate. Serve with chips, vegetables and cheese. Try it with meatballs or cocktail sausages.
Jeri’s Sauce Sour Cream Chili Bake:
  • 1 lb. Ground Beef
  • 1.5 cup Corn Chips
  • 1/2 cup chopped Onion
  • One 16 ounce can Dark Red Kidney Beans, drained
  • 1.5 cup Jeri's Sauce
  • 1 cup grated Cheese
  • 1.25 cup Sour Cream
  • 1 envelope (1-1/2 ounce) Taco Seasoning Mix
  • Brown ground beef with onion, drain. Stir in beans, Jeri's, taco seasoning and 1/4 cup cheese. Place 2-1/2 cup corn chips in bottom of 8"x8"x2" baking dish. Cover with meat mixture and bake at 350° for 20 minutes. Spread sour cream over chili bake and top with remaining cheese and chips. Heat until cheese just melts.
Jeri's’ Yogurt Cream Cheese Dip:
  • One 8 ounce package of softened Cream Cheese
  • 1/2 cup Plain Yogurt
  • 1/8 tsp. Dill Weed
  • 1/2 cup Jeri's Sauce
  • At medium speed beat cream cheese until just smooth. Gradually add the remaining ingredients, until well blended. Pour into serving dish and serve with fresh vegetables.
